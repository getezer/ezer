# Ezer — PRD V3.1

## Problem
- Policyholders don't know what their policy actually covers until a claim is denied
- Most don't understand waiting periods, gaps, or what their real coverage ceiling is
- Even informed users have no guidance on how to improve coverage over time
- Insurers benefit from this asymmetry — Ezer exists to close it

## Target User
- Individual health insurance policyholders
- Long-term policyholders (3+ years) with family coverage
- Users managing elderly parents' policies with PEDs and complex waiting period histories
- Users who have faced partial settlements or cashless rejections

## Core Value — What V3 Delivers Today
- Plain-language insights on active features, gaps, and risks
- Waiting period status by SI block with expiry dates
- Schedule integrity detection (underwriting errors)
- PED quality flags with contestation risk scoring
- Draft letters for insurer correction requests and PED clarifications
- Tells users what to do (action outputs), not just what exists
- Deterministic, human-curated output — no hallucination risk

## What Is Missing (The Gap V3.1 Addresses)
- No view of how the policy changes over time
- No guidance on what to do at renewal vs mid-year
- No way to see what the policy will look like in 12 or 24 months
- No suggestions for making coverage stronger year over year
- Ezer currently answers "what is my situation today" — not "what should I do next"

## V3.1 Goal
- Treat the policy as a living asset, not a static document
- Show how coverage evolves as waiting periods expire
- Surface renewal-time decisions before they arise
- Guide users toward coverage maturity — not just coverage awareness

## Key Capabilities to Build in V3.1

### 1 — Policy Evolution Timeline
- Show what changes at each future milestone (Feb 2027, Feb 2028)
- Which waiting periods expire, which blocks become fully active
- Which PEDs become claimable on which blocks and when

### 2 — Renewal Guidance Layer
- What to add at next renewal and why (Unlimited Restore, Protector Rider)
- Cost-benefit framing for each recommended addition
- Flag decisions that must be made before renewal date (portability window, SI enhancement)

### 3 — Coverage Maturity Score
- Simple 3-state or score-based indicator of how complete the policy is
- Distinguishes gaps that are time-resolving vs gaps requiring action vs gaps requiring renewal decisions
- Trajectory: where will this policy stand in 1 year, 2 years

### 4 — Family Portfolio View (Future)
- Aggregate view across multiple family policies
- Cross-policy gaps and redundancies
- Which family member is most exposed today

## What V3.1 Does NOT Do
- No claim processing or settlement analysis (V4 scope)
- No LLM extraction from raw PDFs
- No new product types beyond existing curated library

## Success Criteria
- User can answer: "What should I do at my next renewal?"
- User can answer: "When will my policy be fully active?"
- User can answer: "How can I make this policy stronger?"
